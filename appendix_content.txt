APPENDIX CONTENT FROM LESSON AND READING GUIDE (Pages 251-258)
================================================================

The appendix contains 4 key pieces:

1. OPEN MIND MEDITATION (261) - Pages 252-253
   A meditation technique for going "beyond words" to direct experience.
   Summary of technique:
   - Repeat the idea for the day as an invitation to God
   - Empty your mind of all words, thoughts, and ideas
   - Hold your mind in a state like a vacuum (negative: empty of words/thoughts; 
     positive: wait expectantly for fullness from God)
   - When mind wanders, use words to draw it back to silent waiting
   - Realize the practice period is in the Holy Spirit's hands
   - Conclude by repeating the idea for the day

2. I NEED DO NOTHING PRAYER - Page 254
   A 9-part prayer with sections:
   1. Father, how still today!
   2. How quietly do all things fall in place!
   3. This is the day...
   "Rest from Doing" section:
   4. In You is every choice already made.
   5. In You has every conflict been resolved.
   6. In You is everything I hope to find already given me.
   "The Peace that Is Mine" section:
   7. Your peace is mine.
   8. My heart is quiet, and my mind at rest.
   9. Your Love is Heaven, and Your Love is mine.

3. PRAYER FOR LESSON 341 - Page 255
   3-part prayer:
   1. Father, Your Son is holy. (And Your Son is me.)
   2. I am he on whom You smile. (Your Smile is everything to me...)
   3. How pure, how safe, how holy, then, are we. (Extended poetic prayer)

4. WHERE DO I GO FROM HERE? - Pages 256-258
   Two questions addressed:
   a) "Should I repeat the Workbook?" 
      - Answer: If you've formed strong habits of daily spiritual practice, 
        you may be ready to set it aside. If not, repeat it.
      - The Workbook is a training camp, not the journey itself.
   
   b) "What do I do after the Workbook?"
      - References Manual for Teachers, Section 16: "How Should the Teacher 
        of God Spend His Day?" (M-16)
      - Post-Workbook practice outline:
        1. Begin the day right, join with God morning (M-16.4:7)
        2. Repeat same procedures at night (5:1)
        3. Remember God all through the day (6:1-14)
        4. Turn to the Holy Spirit with all your problems (7:4-5)
        5. Respond to all temptations by reminding yourself of truth (8:1-3; 10:8; 11:9)
   
   c) "Hints for post-Workbook practice"
      - Make a list of useful thoughts from the Course
      - Study the Text passages in italic type
      - Revisit powerful lessons occasionally
      - Set your own individualized curriculum with the Holy Spirit
      - Continue practices indefinitely until you no longer need them
      
   Final quote: W-pI.153.18:1-4 about walking with God's Voice
